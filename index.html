    What is the AI

How does that improve our lives you might ask yourself? So AI is very useful in many different ways.     Medicine and oncology and why is the AI included

    Oncology, also called Carcinology, is a faculty in medicine. Mainly the goal is to research, investigate and treat cancer. They try to understand the mechanism of formation of the tumors, carcinomas, sarcomas etc. and seek the best means to fight it. Mostly it is done in the following four methods: chemotherapy, hormone therapy, immunotherapy, and specific cancer therapy. The most common, that we are going to take a look at afterwards, is chemotherapy. Why does the AI get included in oncology, and medicine in general? The AI has a bright future in the medical field. It has the capability to fundamentally transform medicine. The AI can provide great support, especially in the oncology. AI have a quick understanding how cancer cells become resistant to their drug, which can help improve the development and adjust the usage of the drug. So in this blog we are taking a look at the AI in chemotherapy and the AI in supervising patients.

    AI in the usage of chemotherapy

    Chemotherapy is the most known and most used cancer treatment. They include the usage of cytotoxic drugs. These drugs involve cell division that leads to the death of the cancer cells. As good as this sounds it has also a negative effect. They also attack the healthy body cells leading to side effects. The AI gets included in the chemotherapy because it adapts the treatment to the individual patient better. Each individual patient has his or her own treatment plan produced by the AI. What is the use of such an individual plan and why should it be used? So this can not only reduce the side effect but it also can increase the effectiveness of the treatment because each patient has its perfectly planned treatment and the patients can get well without the unnecessary consumption of medicine. Using AI in this manner can lead to a drastic decrease of the related waste of medicine.

They have an Video-based AI algorithm to scan the patient's face to make sure, that the drug is ingested. That procedure happens in 8 nanoseconds.
